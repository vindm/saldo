# Checklist: replying to an FTS demand

Applies to any FTS demand: for clarifications, for submission of documents, for a summons, or for payment.

## Stage 1. Receipt and classification

- [ ] Record the date the demand was received (deadlines are counted from it)
- [ ] Read the demand in full, without skipping
- [ ] Determine the type:
  - Demand for submission of documents (Art. 93 of the Tax Code)
  - Demand for submission of clarifications (Art. 88 of the Tax Code)
  - Demand for payment (Art. 69 of the Tax Code)
  - Notice of a tax offense
- [ ] Determine the reply deadline (usually 5 business days — clarifications, 10 business days — documents)
- [ ] Record the deadline in `Consolidated_calendar_2026.xlsx`
- [ ] Inform the client that a demand has arrived

## Stage 2. Analysis

- [ ] Understand what exactly is being demanded (period, documents, transactions)
- [ ] Open the client card, check the tax regime
- [ ] Open `_registries/reporting_archive/` for the period the demand relates to
- [ ] Understand whether there's a real problem or this is an automated demand
- [ ] If a legal interpretation is needed — flag to the supervisor

## Stage 3. Preparing the reply

- [ ] Gather the requested documents (statements, acts, contracts, returns)
- [ ] Make good-quality copies (PDF)
- [ ] Prepare a cover letter
- [ ] If clarifications are requested — compose a clear, factual reply with no fluff
- [ ] Reconcile with the supervisor

## Stage 4. Sign-off

- [ ] Show the client the draft reply
- [ ] Get an OK from the client
- [ ] Get an OK from the supervisor (if the demand is serious)
- [ ] Sign with the client's UKEP

## Stage 5. Sending

- [ ] Send through the filing system (Kontur.Extern / SBIS)
- [ ] Wait for the FTS acceptance receipt
- [ ] Take a screenshot of the receipt

## Stage 6. Closing

- [ ] Put a copy of the demand, the reply, and the receipt in `_registries/reporting_archive/2026/<client>/FTS_demands/<date>/`
- [ ] Record the demand and the reply in the client's `history.md`
- [ ] Close the task in Finkoper
- [ ] If there's a risk of repeat demands — raise a flag in the client card ("Specifics" section)

## What NOT to do

- Don't ignore a demand (penalty for non-submission)
- Don't send documents that were NOT requested (only what's in the demand)
- Don't give clarifications that go beyond the scope of the demand
- Don't miss the deadline
